Problem

Families visiting museums often feel overwhelmed: long routes, unclear wayfinding, and kids losing focus quickly. Existing tools focus on static maps instead of adaptive guidance that reacts to how people move and feel.

Process

I started with short interviews and observations inside the museum, mapping how visitors moved and where they hesitated. This led to user journeys that highlighted pain points like confusing transitions between spaces and a lack of clear “break moments”.

Key steps

  • Quick intercept interviews with visitors and families
  • User journey mapping across arrival, exploration, and exit
  • Sketching possible route types (calm, kid-focused, fast)
  • Wireframes and a clickable prototype to test navigation flows

Solution

The concept app lets visitors choose a route type and then guides them using clear visuals, icons, and optional audio. It highlights rest spots and multi-sensory stops, reducing decision fatigue and making it easier for families to stay engaged together.

Learnings & Next Steps

Testing showed that families liked the idea of clear, planned routes, but also needed flexibility for breaks and detours. Future iterations would include more testing on-site, accessibility settings (e.g. contrast, audio options), and tighter integration with museum content and staff workflows.
